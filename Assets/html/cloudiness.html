Latitude v Cloudiness

There appears to be no apparent correlation between latitude and cloudiness, in the northern hemisphere or southern hemispheres. However, when looking at the graph the cloudiness tends to cluster around certain percentage values, which may be an indicator that cloudiness may be a result of the altitude of a city and less so the latitudes of the cities themselves.

Given how cloudiness appears across a variety of latitudes, cloudiness could be a result of a variety of weather conditions that could allow for cloudiness. Generally cloudiness can be associated with rainier weather or storms. However, warmer weathers can also result in cloudiness.

As such, additional data points may need to be collected in order to determine how cloudiness may be correlated to other locations or weather conditions and patterns.
